# I-765 Category Reference

This file helps identify which I-765 category a user has and what kinds
of evidence typically satisfy an RFE for that category. Always read the
actual RFE to determine the specific items requested — this is a guide,
not a substitute.

---

## F-1 Student Categories (Most Common)

### (c)(3)(A) — F-1 Pre-Completion OPT
Student is in the final year of study and applying for OPT before graduation.

**Common RFE items:**
- I-20 with OPT recommendation
- DSO statement confirming full-time enrollment and OPT recommendation
- Transcripts showing completion of one full academic year
- Proof of upcoming graduation

**Key note:** Pre-completion OPT requires that the student works part-time
(20 hrs/week max) during the school term and full-time during breaks.

---

### (c)(3)(B) — F-1 Post-Completion OPT
Student has graduated and is applying for up to 12 months of OPT.

**Common RFE items:**
- Final I-20 with OPT recommendation dated within 30 days of application
- Transcripts showing degree completion
- DSO letter confirming graduation date
- Proof that the job is related to the degree field
- Address history (5 years)
- Any evidence of prior CPT/OPT used (it reduces available OPT time)

**Key note:** The I-765 must be filed no later than 60 days after graduation
(30 days after the OPT start date on the I-20). Timing errors are a common
RFE trigger.

---

### (c)(3)(C) — F-1 STEM OPT Extension
Graduate with a STEM degree (CIP code list maintained by DHS) applying for
a 24-month extension after initial OPT.

**Common RFE items:**
- I-20 with STEM OPT recommendation
- Form I-983 Training Plan (signed by both student and employer)
- Employer's E-Verify participation confirmation
- Prior EAD (the extension builds on the initial OPT card)
- Job description showing the position requires STEM skills from the degree
- Evidence that employer has a bona fide training relationship

**Key note:** The employer must be E-Verify enrolled. The I-983 is the most
scrutinized document — it must show specific learning objectives, supervision,
and connection to the degree curriculum.

---

### CPT (Curricular Practical Training)
CPT is authorized on the I-20, not the I-765. However, some schools still
require an I-765 for CPT in certain program structures (especially hybrid or
executive programs where the CPT overlaps with enrollment).

**Common RFE items for CPT-related I-765:**
- I-20 showing CPT authorization for the relevant employer and period
- DSO letter explaining the CPT structure and how it satisfies degree requirements
- Enrollment verification showing full-time status (9+ credit hours)
- Syllabi for CPT-authorized courses
- Employer letter connecting job duties to specific coursework
- Evidence of in-seat residency attendance (if hybrid program)
- Full course of study documentation

## Other Common I-765 Categories

### (a)(3) — Refugee
Refugee status holders are eligible to work upon admission.

**Common RFE items:**
- I-94 showing refugee status
- Refugee travel document or equivalent
- Proof of refugee admission (I-730 or equivalent)

---

### (a)(5) — Asylee
Granted asylum in the United States.

**Common RFE items:**
- I-94 or I-797 showing grant of asylum
- Photo ID
- Proof of continuous presence if applicable

---

### (c)(9) — Pending Adjustment of Status (I-485 filed)
Applicant has a pending green card application.

**Common RFE items:**
- I-485 receipt notice (I-797C) showing pending status
- Valid passport
- Proof that underlying immigrant visa petition is still valid
- Photos and biometrics confirmation

**Key note:** The (c)(9) EAD is issued while waiting for the green card.
If the green card is approved, the EAD becomes unnecessary.

---

### (c)(14) — Deferred Action
Includes DACA and other deferred action categories.

**Common RFE items (DACA):**
- Proof of DACA approval (I-797 approval notice)
- Evidence of continuous residence since June 15, 2007
- Evidence of being in the US on June 15, 2012
- School enrollment records
- Tax returns or work history showing presence
- Any criminal history disclosures

---

### (c)(33) — Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)
Specific DACA category. Requirements similar to (c)(14) above but specifically
for individuals brought to the US as children.

---

### (c)(10) — Withholding of Deportation / Convention Against Torture
Similar to asylee category.

**Common RFE items:**
- I-797 or immigration court order granting withholding
- Photo ID

---

## Reading the RFE — Key Extraction Points

When the user shares their RFE letter, extract:

1. **Category code** — appears as "(c)(3)(B)" or similar in the notice
2. **Specific numbered requests** — listed as "1.", "2.", etc. in the body
3. **Deadline** — explicitly stated; calculate business days from RFE date
4. **Service center** — affects mailing address for response
5. **Language about deficiencies** — tells you what USCIS thinks is missing

The RFE will often use phrases like "the record does not establish..." or
"the evidence is insufficient to demonstrate..." — these are signals about
exactly what needs to be addressed.

## Evidence Strength Tiers

When advising on which documents to prioritize:

**Tier 1 — Highest weight (official, hard to forge):**
- Official transcripts with registrar seal
- Government-issued ID and travel documents
- Court orders, I-797 notices
- Bank statements (official, not screenshots)

**Tier 2 — Strong (institutional on letterhead):**
- DSO letters on school letterhead with signature
- Employer verification letters on company letterhead
- Enrollment verification with registrar signature

**Tier 3 — Supporting (contextual):**
- Syllabi, course schedules
- Tuition receipts and payment confirmations
- Hotel and flight receipts
- Photographs with location metadata

**Tier 4 — Explanatory (useful but not standalone):**
- Personal statements and declarations
- Explanatory letters for unusual situations
- Address history affidavits

Always lead with Tier 1–2 evidence and use lower tiers to fill gaps or
provide context for unusual circumstances.
