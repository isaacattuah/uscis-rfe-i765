# User Guide: Responding to a USCIS I-765 RFE

**What this is**: A plain-language guide for anyone who has received a
Request for Evidence (RFE) from USCIS on their I-765 (Employment
Authorization) application, and wants to use AI (Claude or Gemini) to
help build their response.

---

## What Is an RFE?

A Request for Evidence means USCIS reviewed your application and needs more
information before making a decision. It is **not a denial**. You have a
specific deadline to respond — usually 87 days for standard processing or
a shorter window for premium processing cases.

**Your goal**: Submit a well-organized, complete response before the
deadline. Every item USCIS asked for must be addressed.

---

## Before You Start

Gather the following:

**Your RFE letter (I-797C notice)**
This came in the mail. It lists:
- Your receipt number (e.g., IOE9191499596)
- Your A-Number (your USCIS identifier, e.g., A141-078-631)
- The specific items they are requesting (numbered list)
- The response deadline

**Your documents folder**
Organize your files on your computer into rough categories. You don't
have to be perfect — the AI will help you sort them. Common categories:
- School letters and transcripts
- Course materials (syllabi)
- Employment letters
- Bank statements and payment receipts
- Travel receipts (flights, hotels)
- Your I-20 and previous EADs

**Your address history**
Write down every U.S. address you've lived at for the past 5 years:
- Street address (including apartment number)
- City, State, ZIP
- Dates you lived there (month/year to month/year)
- A one-line note (e.g., "college dorm," "first apartment after graduation")

---

## How the AI Helps You

When you share your RFE with the AI, it will:

1. **Read your RFE** and identify exactly what USCIS is asking for
2. **Walk you through documents** — tell you what each item needs and help you match your files to the requests
3. **Build a professional PDF packet** organized into labeled tabs (Tab A, Tab B, etc.) that match USCIS expectations
4. **Write your cover letter** — a multi-page letter explaining your case, your address history, any travel, and why you qualify
5. **Check the packet** before you submit — verifying that every document is present and correctly labeled
6. **Guide you through submission** — online via myaccount.uscis.gov or by mail

---

## The Tab System

Your response will be organized into lettered tabs, each covering a
different topic. Standard tabs for F-1 students (OPT/CPT):

| Tab | Contents |
|-----|----------|
| A | USCIS Filing Documents (your I-20s, prior EADs, receipt notices, passport) |
| B | School Letters (DSO statement, registrar letter, enrollment verification) |
| C | Course of Study (transcripts, course schedule, degree requirements) |
| D | Course Syllabi |
| E | Attendance Records |
| F | Tuition Payment Receipts |
| G | Bank Statements |
| H | Travel and Lodging Receipts (for in-person residencies or classes) |
| I | Employment Documentation (offer letter, employment verification, job description) |

The specific tabs will be adjusted based on what your RFE asks for.

## What You Need to Do (vs. What the AI Does)

**You do:**
- Collect and provide the actual documents
- Confirm that all facts in the cover letter are accurate
- Review the final packet before submitting
- **Sign the cover letter** (print, sign, scan — or type `/s/ Your Full Name`)
- Submit to USCIS

**The AI does:**
- Read and interpret your RFE
- Organize documents into tabs
- Create professional tab cover pages with color coding
- Write your cover letter based on your specific situation
- Build all PDFs automatically
- Create an addressed envelope label
- Double-check everything before you submit

## Step-by-Step Process

### Step 1: Share your RFE
Upload your RFE letter to the AI, or paste the key details (receipt number,
deadline, list of items requested).

### Step 2: Answer questions
The AI will ask about your background, address history, travel, and
employment. Answer as accurately as possible — these details go into
your cover letter.

### Step 3: Provide your documents
Share the files the AI asks for. You can upload PDFs, photos (JPG/PNG),
or describe what you have.

### Step 4: Review the cover letter
Read the generated cover letter carefully. Check:
- Is every fact correct? (Names, dates, addresses, dollar amounts)
- Are all your documents listed in the enclosures?
- Does the letter address every item USCIS requested?

### Step 5: Sign and submit
Print the signature page, sign it, scan it back in. Then upload your
complete packet to myaccount.uscis.gov or mail it to the address on
your RFE letter.

## Important Reminders

**Deadlines are absolute.** If you miss the response deadline, USCIS
will decide your case without your evidence, which usually means denial.
Submit at least a few days before the deadline to be safe.

**Keep copies of everything.** Before you submit, save a copy of your
complete packet. If USCIS loses something, you'll need to show what
you sent.

**The signature matters.** Your cover letter contains a declaration
under penalty of perjury. You must sign it before submitting.

**The approval email is not enough.** If you are approved, you will
eventually receive a physical I-797 Approval Notice in the mail. This
is your actual work authorization document. Give a copy to your HR
department.

**Online submission is faster.** If your case is on myaccount.uscis.gov,
upload your response there rather than mailing it. Online submissions
are tracked and confirmed immediately.

---

## When to Get a Lawyer

Consider consulting an immigration attorney if:
- You have a prior visa violation or status gap
- You were previously denied or placed in removal proceedings
- You are unsure whether your employer's CPT/OPT setup was compliant
- Your situation is unusual in any way not covered by the RFE

The AI can help organize documents and write clearly, but it cannot
give you legal advice or predict USCIS decisions.

---

## After You Submit

- You will receive an email confirmation (if submitted online)
- Check your case status at uscis.gov/casestatus
- For premium processing: decision within 15 business days of receipt
- For standard processing: check uscis.gov/processing-times

If USCIS approves your case, watch the mail for your physical EAD card
and the I-797 Approval Notice. Both will come separately.
